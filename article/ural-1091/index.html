题解 - [Ural 1091] Tmutarakan Exams

发表于 2020-07-31 更新于 2020-10-23 分类于 程序设计 ， 题解 阅读次数：

题目链接

原始题面

University of New Tmutarakan trains the first-class specialists in mental arithmetic. To enter the University you should master arithmetic perfectly. One of the entrance exams at the Divisibility Department is the following. Examinees are asked to find KKK different numbers that have a common divisor greater than 111. All numbers in each set should not exceed a given number SSS. The numbers KKK and SSS are announced at the beginning of the exam. To exclude copying (the Department is the most prestigious in the town!) each set of numbers is credited only once (to the person who submitted it first).

Last year these numbers were K=25K=25K=25 and S=49S=49S=49 and, unfortunately, nobody passed the exam. Moreover, it was proved later by the best minds of the Department that there do not exist sets of numbers with the required properties. To avoid embarrassment this year, the dean asked for your help. You should find the number of sets of K different numbers, each of the numbers not exceeding SSS, which have a common divisor greater than 111. Of course, the number of such sets equals the maximal possible number of new students of the Department.

Input

The input contains numbers KKK and SSS (2≤K≤S≤502 ≤ K ≤ S ≤ 502≤K≤S≤50).

Output

You should output the maximal possible number of the Department’s new students if this number does not exceed 100001000010000 which is the maximal capacity of the Department, otherwise you should output 100001000010000.

Sample

input output
3 10 11

Problem Author: Stanislav Vasilyev
Problem Source: USU Open Collegiate Programming Contest March’2001 Senior Session

题意简述

给出KKK和SSS, 求在[1,S][1,S][1,S]内互不相同的KKK个数的最大公约数不为111的数对个数, 若大于100001000010000则输出100001000010000

解题思路

设num⁡(K,S,d)\operatorname{num}(K,S,d)num(K,S,d)为: [1,S][1,S][1,S]内互不相同的KKK个数的最大公约数为ddd的数对个数

由容斥定理可知, 所求应为

∑i∈{primes less than S}num⁡(K,S,i)−∑i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<jnum⁡(K,S,ij)+...\sum_{i\in\{\text{primes less than }S\}}\operatorname{num}(K,S,i)-\sum_{i,j\in\{\text{primes less than }S\},~i<j}\operatorname{num}(K,S,ij)+... i∈{primes less than S}∑​num(K,S,i)−i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<j∑​num(K,S,ij)+...

我们很容易就能发现

num⁡(K,S,d)=(⌊Sd⌋K)\operatorname{num}(K,S,d)={\lfloor{S\over d}\rfloor\choose K} num(K,S,d)=(K⌊dS​⌋​)

所以所求即为

∑i∈{primes less than S}(⌊Si⌋K)−∑i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<j(⌊Sij⌋K)+...\sum_{i\in\{\text{primes less than }S\}}{\lfloor{S\over i}\rfloor\choose K}-\sum_{i,j\in\{\text{primes less than }S\},~i<j}{\lfloor{S\over ij}\rfloor\choose K}+... i∈{primes less than S}∑​(K⌊iS​⌋​)−i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<j∑​(K⌊ijS​⌋​)+...

然而, 我们注意到S⩽50S\leqslant 50S⩽50, 而且最小的三素数乘积2×3×5=302\times 3\times 5=302×3×5=30, 所以

∀i,j,k∈{prime}, num⁡(K,S,ijk)=0\forall i,j,k\in\{\text{prime}\},~\operatorname{num}(K,S,ijk)=0 ∀i,j,k∈{prime}, num(K,S,ijk)=0

故所求即为

∑i∈{primes less than S}(⌊Si⌋K)−∑i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<j(⌊Sij⌋K)\sum_{i\in\{\text{primes less than }S\}}{\lfloor{S\over i}\rfloor\choose K}-\sum_{i,j\in\{\text{primes less than }S\},~i<j}{\lfloor{S\over ij}\rfloor\choose K} i∈{primes less than S}∑​(K⌊iS​⌋​)−i,j∈{primes less than S}, i<j∑​(K⌊ijS​⌋​)

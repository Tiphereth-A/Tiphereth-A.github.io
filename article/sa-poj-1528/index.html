原始题面

Description

From the article Number Theory in the 1994 Microsoft Encarta: ``If a,b,ca, b, ca,b,c are integers such that a=bca = bca=bc, aaa is called a multiple of bbb or of ccc, and bbb or ccc is called aaa divisor or factor of aaa. If c is not 111/−1-1−1, bbb is called a proper divisor of aaa. Even integers, which include 000, are multiples of 222, for example, −4,0,2,10-4, 0, 2, 10−4,0,2,10; an odd integer is an integer that is not even, for example, −5,1,3,9-5, 1, 3, 9−5,1,3,9. A perfect number is a positive integer that is equal to the sum of all its positive, proper divisors; for example, 666, which equals 1+2+31 + 2 + 31+2+3, and 282828, which equals 1+2+4+7+141 + 2 + 4 + 7 + 141+2+4+7+14, are perfect numbers. A positive number that is not perfect is imperfect and is deficient or abundant according to whether the sum of its positive, proper divisors is smaller or larger than the number itself. Thus, 999, with proper divisors 1,31, 31,3, is deficient; 121212, with proper divisors 1,2,3,4,61, 2, 3, 4, 61,2,3,4,6, is abundant."

Given a number, determine if it is perfect, abundant, or deficient.

Input

A list of NNN positive integers (none greater than 60,00060,00060,000), with 1<=N<1001 <= N < 1001<=N<100. A 000 will mark the end of the list.

Output

The first line of output should read PERFECTION OUTPUT. The next NNN lines of output should list for each input integer whether it is perfect, deficient, or abundant, as shown in the example below. Format counts: the echoed integers should be right justified within the first 555 spaces of the output line, followed by two blank spaces, followed by the description of the integer. The final line of output should read END OF OUTPUT.

Sample Input

15 28 6 56 60000 22 496 0

Sample Output

PERFECTION OUTPUT
   15  DEFICIENT
   28  PERFECT
    6  PERFECT
   56  ABUNDANT
60000  ABUNDANT
   22  DEFICIENT
  496  PERFECT
END OF OUTPUT

Source

Mid-Atlantic 1996

题意简述

给一组数, 判断是完全(因子和为自身的二倍)的, 充裕(因子和大于自身的二倍)的, 还是不足(因子和小于自身的二倍)的
